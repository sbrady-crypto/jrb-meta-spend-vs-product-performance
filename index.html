Data Notes

How to read this.

  • Meta spend is filtered by product keyword in the ad name (e.g. miraclebalm, herokit, wtf, jetm). Campaigns containing returning, viewcontent, or quiz are excluded.
  • New-customer revenue is Shopify net_sales filtered to new_or_returning_customer = 'new'. Orders are orders_first_time.
  • Bundle SKUs (Complexion Set, Hero Kit) report $0 net_sales in Shopify because revenue flows to the component line items — they ship with the underlying products counted separately. Use their order counts and Meta-side purchases instead.
  • Shared-product ad names: Miracle Balm vs. Mini Miracle Balm are disambiguated by the mini prefix; Bronzer excludes cocoabronze (a Miracle Balm shade) and pinkybronze (ditto); Mascara excludes minimascara (bundled giveaway).
  • Classic Lip has zero Meta spend in this 12-week window — no ads in the classiclip naming convention ran through the filter.
  • Launches inside the window: Mini Miracle Balm and Eyeshadow Stick ramped Feb 9–Mar 2; Lip Recharge scaled from Mar 16; Blushing Stick launched 2026-04-13 (last week). WoW and 4W deltas for these should be read with launch context.
  • Attribution: Meta-side revenue is last-touch from Facebook's own attribution — it doesn't match Shopify 1:1. This dashboard uses Shopify new-customer net sales as the revenue source of truth; Meta spend is the spend source of truth.
  • NC-MER excludes LTV, repeat purchases, and cross-sell. A low NC-MER on a hero product (e.g. Miracle Balm) may still be profitable over the customer lifetime — treat this as a directional signal on acquisition efficiency, not final P&L.
